Put your SIMs cards in the cloud !

Use your Raspberry PI as a GSM gateway.

Install now

What do you need ?

• A 3G USB dongle, if you have one it is probably compatible.

• Something running Linux, a raspberry Pi, an old laptop, anything with USB port will do.

• A SIM card, not a particular one, just a SIM card.

Truly plug and play

No port forwarding to setup, no configuration files to edit. Install semasim on your RaspberryPi with a simple command, connect a 3G dongle with a SIM inside, wait 10second and you will be prompted to enter the PIN code of your SIM.

Any devices

Semasim give you full access to the SIM you have connected to your raspberry via a web application and a mobile app. Voice calls and SMS are both supported.

Multiple SIM on a single device.

Have a business sim and a personal sim but your high end smartphone is not dual SIM? Put one of your card in your phone and connect the other to your raspberry pi and you're good to go. No need for an extra phone.

Bypass roaming fees

Using your personal SIM card from a foreign country can be extremely expensive. With Semasim you can leave the country your SIM won't travel with you. Even if your are at the other side of the word as far as your telecom operator is concern you will be doing national calls. Of course you will need a connection to internet to be able to use your sim. ChatSim can help you with that.

Share your sim SIM

Semasim let you share your share SIMs with coworkers. When two person share a SIM they both receive the incoming call and SMS.
